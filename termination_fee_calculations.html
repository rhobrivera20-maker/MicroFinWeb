Scenario 3: Rebate Only (Rule of 78s) rebate_only

Setup: Flat rate loan with Rule of 78s rebate - borrower pays off after 3 months

Rule of 78s Calculation (Step by Step):

Step 1: Calculate sum of digits for 6 months
6 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 21

Step 2: Allocate interest to each month
- Month 1: 6/21 × ₱180 = ₱51.43
- Month 2: 5/21 × ₱180 = ₱42.86
- Month 3: 4/21 × ₱180 = ₱34.29
- Month 4: 3/21 × ₱180 = ₱25.71
- Month 5: 2/21 × ₱180 = ₱17.14
- Month 6: 1/21 × ₱180 = ₱8.57
Total: ₱180.00

Step 3: Calculate earned interest (months 1-3)
₱51.43 + ₱42.86 + ₱34.29 = ₱128.58

Step 4: Calculate unearned interest (rebate)
Total interest (₱180) - Earned interest (₱128.58) = ₱51.42

Why this is called "Rule of 78s":
The sum 6+5+4+3+2+1 = 21, but in a 12-month loan it would be 78 (12+11+10+...+1). The method front-loads interest so early payments have more interest than later payments.

What is Rebated Amount?
This is the amount the borrower would pay if there were NO additional fee. It's the remaining balance minus the unearned interest rebate.
